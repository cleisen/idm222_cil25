EURONEWS WEBSITE
NAVIGATION REDESIGN

Euronews.com is a news site that provides an international view, giving a new and different perspective. The main goal of their site is to deliver a neutral and objective view of the world's news to everyone. This is important to their brand as the users who visit the Euronews website are from across the globe. Below is a user persona that I used as a reference throughout the design process which highlights certain goals a user of Euronews may have.

01
planning
Immediate Goals

After quickly reviewing the home & article pages, I Immediately knew that there were some organizational and overcrowding issues.

I then took the skeleton of each of the pages and made content wireframes to get an idea of how everything was organized.

As you can tell, there is a lot going on. However, before I could determine the main issues I wanted to address in my redesign, I decided to perform an in-depth analysis in order to figure out the leading problems.

Heuristic Evaluation Process

Through a heuristic evaluation process, a peer and I rated certain elements of the Euronews website on a scale of 0-4 on their success. Things that scored a zero were deemed a usability catastrophe and were imperative to fix before the design could be released. Design elements that scored a four were believed to not have a usability problem at all.

As seen in the image below, the areas that had the most issues were the navigation of the site, the home page, the visual design, and the user task orientation.

After evaluating the results and looking back on my user persona, I combined all of the biggest issues and made a list of my main goals for the redesign of Euronews. (seen below)

02
designing
Sketching/Wireframing Process

In an effort to give Euronews a specific identity, I wanted to incorporate circles into the design. The idea was to resemble a globe and tie in the focus of world news in an intriguing way.

During my wireframe sketching process, I did some research along the way. I took ideas from different websites and noticed how they accomplished designs that were simple, intuitive, and unique. In my sketches, I combined my ideas and my research and came up with 16 different layout options.

Final Sketches

I chose this sketch as my final design because it has a good balance of simplicity, creativity, and organization. The articles displayed on the home page are well balanced, but maintain a hierarchy for the lead article. The home page is also organized in a fashion that is uncluttered and continues to contain all the information needed for quick navigation, which was one of the main issues on the original site.

Color Theme

Euronews' core values are focused on honesty, integrity, and neutrality. In order to create a color palette that would reflect those ideas into a visceral feeling for the users, I stuck with variations of blue throughout the design as it is a color of trust, loyalty, and logic.

For the secondary colors, each topic was assigned its own bright color to add variation and easy identification of groupings.

Key Elements

In the end, my final designs had reached the goals I set out to accomplish. The visual design, the organization, and the navigation are much more intuitive and engaging as a whole.

Looking at the video walkthroughs of the final designs, it is apparent that the search function is now easily accessible and consistently in the same relative space within each different dimension. This is helpful so users can easily move between devices without needing to relearn a different way of finding information that needs to be quickly accessed.

In continuing to follow my user personas goals for the site, I decided to add an interactive way for users to navigate to the news they want by country. First, they click or tap on the continent they want to find news on, and it will guide them to a country list within that continent. This allows users to have another place to search for the news they want but in a more advanced and interactive way.

Another small, but very important, key element in the final design is the language choosing tab. Because Euronews is a world news site that broadcasts in 13 different languages, I felt that it was necessary to work this into my design on each of the homepages so users can easily change it as soon as they open the site.

One of the major changes in design was that I completely got rid of the endless list of articles on the original homepage, and simply organized all of the articles by topics. These topics are accessible in the top menu as well as the bottom of the homepage. I believe that the original site was overcrowded, which interrupted navigation for users. Now, the user can either be influenced to choose one of the lead articles advertised at the top of the page, or if they are searching for news on a certain topic, they can quickly go there as well.
